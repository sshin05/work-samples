HOW TO TABLESCAPE THE BEAUX WAY

Last spring, doing hair just fell flat for 35-year-old Ontario stylist Brock Stacey. So he turned his attention to another longtime love — flowers — and quickly made a brand that extolls the power of flowers for all.

THE BEAUX FLORALS APPROACH TO RETAIL WAS AS UNCONVENTIONAL as the arrangements themselves. Brock sold them curbside, first come, first served, from outside his Burlington, Ont. home. By Mother’s Day, what had begun as 10 or 20 arrangements a weekend snowballed into 200 cars queued up for his now Instagram-famous beauxquets. Today it’s events and collaborations that keep him busy, including creating transformative tablescapes for those whose summer plans are small but whose wanderlust has no bounds. We asked Brock to give us some insight into creating the kinds of settings that give us all the vacation vibes.

1. Modern Cottage

“This is all about taking pretty, traditional items and making them cool,” says Brock, who paired Mikasa’s Violetta plates with the GlucksteinHome pinstripe cotton table runner and placemats to achieve this unexpectedly chic pairing of stripes and flowers. “This is ideal for an outdoor lunch in the country – or when you want to feel like you’ve escaped to the country!” Never one to insist on a specialty vessel for his work, he suggests using drinking glasses in lieu of small vases. Consider treating yourself to fragrant lilacs, but don’t overthink it either. A fruit bowl centrepiece, full of grapes and pears, works just fine.

2. Low-Key Beach Vibes

Achieve a laid-back “I never sweat the details” feel with muted, sunwashed hues. It’s all made possible with skillful colour combinations: in this case, a lavender-blue and orangey-peach. “Remember third grade and look right across the colour-wheel for a great pairing,” says Brock. Here, he echoed the blue from the GlucksteinHome Fauna placemats in the flowers and added peach blooms for the perfect foil. “The glassware is a mix, but that works,” he says. All-white tableware and candles keep everything crisp and cohesive. The final element is a knotted napkin – unexpected but easy to do. “Start by making an accordion fold and then simply knot it.”

3. City Soul

There’s never a wrong night for an asymmetrical arrangement, says Brock, but for this one, he’s picturing a rainy, moody evening with those nearest and dearest. The center arrangement is given a bit more oomph with an ingenious pedestal: a stack of plates. “Always start with what you have,” says Brock. Silver Georg Jensen candle-holders, used here as objet, add a bit of glint. The matte-black plates feel supremely urban and have the added benefit of making food look extra appealing. “Once again, it’s colour-contrast at play,” says Brock.

A pro tip you can’t see? It’s called Flower Water and it contains a teaspoon each of Sprite (or sugar) and bleach. The latter wards off bacteria, which Brock says shortens the lifespan of flowers. “And don’t forget to change the water every day, it’s worth it!”
