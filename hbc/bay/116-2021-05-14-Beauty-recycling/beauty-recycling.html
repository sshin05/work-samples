How to Recycle
Your Beauty
Products

So much of our beauty products end up in landfills, even when they’re thrown in the recycling bin. That’s because some things—like pumps and mascara tubes—can’t be recycled.

We’re excited to launch a new program that makes it easier for you to recycle the hard-to-recycle.

Here’s how it works: Empty, rinse and dry your products. They must be less than 60 mL (about the size of a yogurt cup). Then look for the bins in our beauty department.

What We Take

Hair

  • Shampoo and conditioner caps and pumps
  • Travel-size hair styling bottles, tubes and caps

Makeup

  • Mascara tubes
  • Lip product tubes
  • Makeup pencils, sticks and caps
  • Foundation/concealer tubes, bottles and jars
  • Foundation pumps and caps
  • Compacts and palettes

Body

  • Body wash and lotion dispenser pumps

Skin Care

  • Bottles, tubes, dispensers and plastic jars less than 60 mL

What We Don’t Take

  • Aluminum tubes
  • Toothpaste tubes and toothbrushes
  • Nail polish and nail polish remover
  • Dropper tops
  • Beauty sponges
  • Fragrance bottles
  • Extruded glass, like small sample vials
  • Aerosol cans
  • Hair and skin care tools
