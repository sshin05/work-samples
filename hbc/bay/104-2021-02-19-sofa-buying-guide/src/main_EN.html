design do’s

1.

Maximize seating area by choosing a sofa that takes up two-thirds of your wall space.

2.

Lighten a smaller space with upholstery on higher legs.

3.

For a larger room, place your upholstery in groupings to create intimate conversation spaces, for example, two sofas across from each other.

4.

Add some fun to your space by mixing up finishes, fabrics and textures.

5.

Monochromatic rooms work best with neutral colours. Play with scale and textures for maximum elegance.

DON’T FORGET THE MEASUREMENTS

Before buying any type of furniture, make sure to measure your space, including door and hallway widths. Use masking tape to outline the pieces on your floor to ensure the size works for you.

Choose a Material

LEATHER

Top-grain leather is easy to clean, and shows all the natural scars and characteristics of the hide.

VELVET

This fabric offers a comfortable, plush feel and a luxurious look. Vibrant colours add depth to any room.

Woven and Linen Textured Fabrics

Simple and elegant, these materials are breathable and classically laid-back. Perfect for furniture with clean lines.

chenille

This pile fabric feels similar to corduroy and velvet. Soft to the touch, durable and wrinkle-resistant.

boucle

Created by weaving curly yarns together, this material is soft, rich in colour and hides wear well.

Performance Fabrics

Made from solution-dyed acrylics, these easy to clean and durable materials are great for families with kids or pets.

3 Ways to Care for Your Sofa

Caring for your sofa is the key to keeping it looking brand new.

1.

Dust and dirt can cause fabric upholstery to wear faster, so regularly vacuum with an upholstery attachment (and professionally cleaned once a year).

2.

Flip, rotate and fluff removable cushions to maintain shape.

3.

Keep your sofa away from direct sunlight and heat sources to prevent fading.

Bonus Tip for Leather Sofas:

To minimize leather puddling, smooth the surface and tuck excess leather into sides and backs.
