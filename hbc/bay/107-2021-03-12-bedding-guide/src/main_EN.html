fill power

Personalize your duvet by choosing a comfortable fill power that measures the feather or down's fluffiness.

Facts:

The higher the fill power, the larger the down clusters and the better the quality.

The better the quality, the longer it will retain its firmness.

The larger the down cluster, the better the insulating power and the lighter the duvet.

What Do These Duvet Terms Mean?

“the fill”

This refers to the material inside your duvet. Warmth, breathability and weight are all affected by your choice of fill.

“the shell"

This is the fabric that holds and protects the fill. For a down duvet, make sure the shell is “down-proof” to prevent the feathers from poking through.

“Baffle Box Construction”

A method in which thin strips of material are sewn between the top and bottom cover, creating stitched squares. This ensures even distribution of fill.

“the loft"

Also known as volume or fill power, this refers to how many cubic inches 1 ounce of down will fill. The higher the number (look for a loft of at least 500), the fluffier the duvet.

“piping"

A duvet with a piped finish means the shell is outlined with a threaded weave that resembles a decorative cord. This ensures the duvet border is securely enclosed.

caring for your sheets

General Washing and Drying Tips:

Do not overcrowd the washing machine.
Use a gentle cycle and warm water to prevent colour from fading.
Remove wet sheets quickly after wash to avoid mold or excessive wrinkles.
Wash sheets weekly, alternating between two or more sets to preserve quality.
When possible, air dry on a clothesline (if using a dryer, use medium or low setting).

Different fabrics need different care, so always read the instructions on the label.

Silk and Sateen
  • Use detergent made for delicate fabrics
  • Gentle setting, cold water
  • Hang to dry
Cotton
  • Use regular detergent
  • Warm water
  • Hang to dry or use low/medium dryer setting
Linen/tencel
  • Use natural detergent
  • Cold water
  • Low dryer setting
Bamboo
  • Use mild detergent
  • Cold water
  • Avoid using bleach
  • Hang to dry

Caring for Your Pillows and Duvets

Keep Them Light and Fluffy

  • Shake, pat and fluff your pillows and duvets every day to maintain the density of the filling
  • Don't vacuum your duvets to avoid damage
  • To make the filling light and airy again, throw them in the dryer with a few tennis balls for 15-20 minutes
  • It's best for natural-filled pillows and duvets to be professionally laundered

Washing and Drying Tips:

  • Put synthetic pillows in the washing machine, one at a time
  • Remember, foam can't be washed. If needed, wipe lightly with a damp cloth and then use a dry cloth immediately after cleaning
  • Pillows with latex filling can be washed in a full washing machine and centrifuged at max. 1000 rpm (keep pillow cover), then tumble dried

Signs It's Time for a New Pillow or Duvet:

  • They've gone yellow
  • They've gone flat or the filling clumps after being in the dryer
  • Change your pillows and duvets more often if you tend to get hot at night
