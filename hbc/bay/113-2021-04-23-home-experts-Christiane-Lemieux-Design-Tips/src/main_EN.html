3 Design Tips

  1. Paint is the easiest and the most
    transformative thing for a room. Bring
    outdoorelements and colours inside for
    a groundingand balancing natural palette.
  2. My “360 Degrees of Home” practice
    identifies elements that make us feel good
    in our homes based on the five senses.
    Are you a soft rug underfoot person? A crisp
    sheets lover? What feels best to you?
  3. Say “yes” to the seasonal.
    For example, try swapping out your wool
    pillows for linen ones in the spring.

3 Design Dont's

  1. Don’t get stuck in work-at-home mode.
    At the end of the day, you need to be able
    to decompress and relax. Bringing work
    into bed? Well, it happens, but if you can
    keep your bedroom a sacred space,
    that’s a design win right now.
  2. Don’t give weight to trends.
    Do what feels good to you.
  3. Don’t minimize the importance of sleep.
    Sleep hygiene — and a gorgeously dressed
    bed in which to luxuriate and relax
    — is not only a design “yes,” it’s good
    for your own health!

Christiane’s Fav 2021 Trend

“My “360 of Home” concept, based on how we experience our environment with the five senses, is not really a trend — it’s a way of life. Now, and going forward into 2021, the 360 of Home is not just the new sofa you finally purchased after fantasizing for years (sight) or a beautifully scented candle you burn to relax (smell) or the bedding that makes you feel rested and rejuvenated (touch). The 360 of Home hinges on the sixth sense — Overall, how does your home make you feel? It’s not trend-based; rather, it’s truly personal.”

— Christiane Lemieux
